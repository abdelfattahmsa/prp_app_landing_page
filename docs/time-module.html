Time Module

Time Module Guide

Master calendar events, time blocking, task management, and schedule allocation reports to own your day.

On this page

Overview Calendar & Events Task Management Time Tracking Schedule Reports Tips & Best Practices

Overview

The Time module is your command center for everything time-related. It combines a smart calendar, a task manager, and a built-in time tracker — all in one view so you spend less time switching tools and more time getting things done.

Calendar & Events

The calendar displays your day, week, and month views. To add an event:

  1. Tap a time slot on the calendar grid, or press the + button.
  2. Enter an event title, start time, end time, and optional notes.
  3. Choose a color label to categorize the event (work, personal, health, etc.).
  4. Tap Save — the event syncs instantly across all your devices.
Tip: Long-press any event to drag and reschedule it directly on the calendar grid.

Task Management

Tasks live alongside your calendar so due dates are always in context. Each task supports:

  • Priority levels — High, Medium, Low with color coding.
  • Due dates — Tasks with due dates appear on your calendar automatically.
  • Subtasks — Break big tasks into actionable steps stored as JSONB.
  • Notes — Add context or links to any task.

Time Tracking

The built-in timer lets you track exactly how long you spend on any task or project:

  1. Open a task and tap the Start Timer button.
  2. A floating timer banner appears at the top of every screen so it's always visible.
  3. Tap Stop when you're done — the elapsed time is logged to that task.
  4. Review total time per task in the weekly schedule allocation chart.
The floating focus timer also works with the Energy module's Pomodoro sessions — both share the same persistent banner so you never have two timers competing.

Schedule Reports

Head to Time → Reports to see how you actually spent your week:

  • Schedule allocation chart — Pie/bar breakdown of time by category.
  • Daily totals — Hours logged per day over the past 7 or 30 days.
  • Top tasks — Which tasks consumed the most time.

Tips & Best Practices

  • Start each morning by reviewing your calendar and setting 1–3 priority tasks.
  • Use color labels consistently — e.g. green for deep work, blue for meetings.
  • Run the weekly report every Sunday to identify where your time actually goes vs. where you planned it to go.
  • Pair time blocking on the calendar with the Energy module's Pomodoro timer for maximum focus.
